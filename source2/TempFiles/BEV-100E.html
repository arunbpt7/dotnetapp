For your application for cash assistance, the Bureau of Eligibility Verification’s Real Property and Asset Unit must review certain documents related to property you own.
Under the New York State Social Services Law, Sections 106 and 360, HRA must place a Bond and Mortgage on your property, or if your property is a co-op apartment, file a Uniform Commercial Code lien (UCC-1).  
To complete this process, you must come to the BEV Real Property and Asset Unit for an interview.   If you are not the owner of the property, you can explain this at the interview.
